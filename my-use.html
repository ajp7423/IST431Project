How I Use ChatGPT

Academic Use

In academic contexts, I use ChatGPT mainly for refining my writing and brainstorming. I use it to clarify and strengthen my word choice, generate initial ideas or outlines for assignments, and evaluate whether the structure of my writing makes sense. I do not use it to complete assignments. It instead functions as a tool I use at the beginning and end of my writing.

For example, I had an assignment where I had to write about ten of the most important cyberattacks and I used ChatGPT to give me a preliminary list that I dove deeper into myself. Another example is for a geography paper where I had to use environmental ethics to determine why I would or would not build a solar power plant in a town. I performed my own research and wrote the paper and then used ChatGPT to ensure I used strong verbs and wording and that my argument made sense.

However, I do use ChatGPT in accordance with my professors' policies. Different professors allow different levels of AI, and I adjust how I use it to be in line with those policies. Going back to the cyberattacks assignment example, that professor is very strict about the use of AI for writing assignments, and so I used ChatGPT only to get an idea of events to write cyberattacks before doing my research and writing my paper, and did not use it again. So the context of my ChatGPT use in academic settings varies based on each professor's policies.

ChatGPT performs well in this context when generating in-depth outlines or suggesting stronger wording. However, its responses can sometimes be generic or incorrect, which requires one to closely evaluate what it generates. Its outputs are rarely usable after just a few prompts and revisions and independent research are always necessary. An unintended consequence of using it in academic writing is the potential to rely too heavily on it instead of fully researching and articulating ideas by oneself. Balancing it as an assistant and not using it as a shortcut for completing assignments is crucial.

Personal Use

Outside of school, I use ChatGPT primarily for coding assistance, self-teaching, and professional development tasks such as drafting cover letters and refining my resume. Design and programming is not my academic focus, but I still want to have a foundation in those skills so I am teaching myself them. ChatGPT is one of many resources I use to teach myself those concepts and it is my primary tool for debugging and enhancing my code. In this context, it functions as a learning guide that helps me understand and apply complex concepts.

I also use ChatGPT to help structure and refine professional materials. For example, I use it to make bullet points on my resume sound more technical or fancy. I also use it to draft cover letters or help tailor a previous cover letter to match a similar position in a different company. In this context, all of the experiences and information come from me, but ChatGPT helps make the tone and wording more professional and match with specific job expectations.

ChatGPT is useful for explaining programming concepts clearly. It saves me from scrolling through Stack Overflow forums that just make me more confused. It also helps make professional writing sound more polished. However, similar to the academic context, it is not always precise. Generated code may not always do what I want it do and resume phrasing can sound generic without enough information. An unintended consequence of using it in personal contexts is the risk of relying too heavily on it for help, which could limit problem-solving ability or being able to express oneself.
