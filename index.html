Introduction

ChatGPT is a generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) system developed by OpenAI. It interacts with users to generate content such as text, images, code, and even audio conversations. It uses knowledge gained in its training process to predict the sequence of words in its responses based on patterns in its training data. This system has made ChatGPT become a widely used tool for writing assistance, coding support, studying, and general knowledge-seeking. However, concerns have also been raised about the ethics of ChatGPT, due to its tendency to hallucinate, incorporate bias, and as well as the environmental effects of GenAI and the consequences of integrating ChatGPT into everyday life.


In this project and through this site, I analyze how I use ChatGPT in two contexts within my personal life: as a writing and brainstorming assistant in my coursework, and as a coding and learning tool in my personal projects. In the academic context, I use it to generate ideas and refine my writing. In the non-academic context, I use it to learn programming concepts and to help me write and debug code. By focusing on these uses, I examine how ChatGPT impacts or is impacted by bias, media manipulation/misinformation, and digital identity, as well as the way it automates social processes and how it supports human-technology collaboration.
